What Is It

What Is a HUD
& FHA Inspection?

An FHA inspection is a property condition assessment required by the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) for homes being purchased with an FHA-backed mortgage. Unlike a standard home inspection, which is performed for the buyer's benefit, an FHA inspection evaluates the property against HUD's Minimum Property Standards — ensuring the home is safe, sound, and secure enough to qualify for federal loan backing.

If your lender requires an FHA inspection on a property in Spring Hill, Brooksville, Weeki Wachee, or anywhere in Hernando County, we provide a thorough, same-day report that meets HUD requirements and keeps your closing on track.

Roof Condition & Remaining Life

HUD requires the roof to have a remaining useful life of at least two years and must not allow moisture entry. We document the roofing material, visible condition, and any deficiencies that could affect FHA eligibility — one of the most common reasons properties fail an FHA inspection in Hernando County.

Structural Soundness

HUD requires that the home's foundation and overall structure be free of conditions that present safety hazards or significant deterioration. We evaluate visible foundation elements, framing, and structural components against the standards required for FHA loan approval.

Working Utilities & Mechanical Systems

All utilities must be on and functional at the time of inspection. We verify that electrical, plumbing, and HVAC systems are in working order. FHA will not approve a loan on a property with non-functional utilities or major mechanical failures.

Health & Safety Hazards

HUD's Minimum Property Standards specifically address health and safety. We evaluate for lead paint hazards on pre-1978 homes, adequate egress from every bedroom, safe handrails and stairways, and absence of conditions presenting immediate hazard to occupants.

HUD's Three Requirements

What Every FHA Property
Must Be

HUD evaluates every FHA-financed property against three core standards. Our inspection documents each one clearly — giving your lender exactly what they need to move forward.

01

Safe

The property must not present health or safety hazards to occupants. This includes electrical safety, adequate egress, handrails, lead paint on pre-1978 homes, and absence of environmental hazards.

02

Sound

The property must be structurally sound with no conditions that compromise the integrity of the foundation, framing, roof, or major systems. Deferred maintenance that threatens structural integrity must be addressed.

03

Secure

The property must provide adequate protection against the elements. All exterior doors, windows, and the roof must function properly and prevent moisture or pest entry.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions
About HUD & FHA Inspections

Is an FHA inspection the same as a home inspection?

No. An FHA inspection evaluates the property against HUD's Minimum Property Standards for loan approval. A full home inspection is a more comprehensive evaluation performed for the buyer's benefit. Both are recommended.

What does an FHA inspection look for?

Health and safety hazards, structural soundness, and adequate property security — including roof condition, functioning utilities, lead paint on pre-1978 homes, and safe egress from every bedroom.

What happens if a property fails an FHA inspection?

Required repairs must be completed before the loan can be approved. Our report documents all deficiencies clearly, giving buyers and sellers a specific list of items to address before closing.

Can you do both inspections in one visit?

Yes. We can perform both your FHA-required inspection and a full home inspection during the same visit — saving time and helping keep your Hernando County transaction moving forward.
