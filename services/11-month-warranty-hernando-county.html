What’s Included in a Warranty Inspection


An 11-month inspection is a non-invasive, visual examination of the home's accessible areas. You are provided with a report that you can take to the builder and use to capitalize on your home's 1 year warranty before it expires.

Why get an 11-Month Warranty Inspection?


Purchasing a new home is one of life’s biggest investments, but the "honeymoon phase" of the first year can often mask underlying issues that only surface after the house has gone through a full cycle of seasons. Most builders offer a comprehensive one-year limited warranty that covers workmanship and materials, but this protection typically expires on your move-in anniversary. An 11-month warranty inspection serves as your final opportunity to identify defects—such as structural settling cracks, hidden plumbing leaks, or HVAC inefficiencies—and have them repaired at the builder's expense rather than your own.

A professional third-party inspection provides an unbiased, detailed report that holds the builder accountable to the quality standards you were promised. While many issues like "nail pops" or sticking doors seem minor, they can be symptoms of larger concerns like improper grading or foundation movement. By documenting these findings before the 12th month, you gain the necessary leverage to ensure your home is in peak condition, protecting your property value and providing long-term peace of mind before you take full financial responsibility for all future repairs

Drywall & Trim Separation

As the wood framing in a new home dries and the foundation settles, drywall cracks and trim separation are frequent. These are more than just cosmetic; they can indicate areas where the home is moving more than expected.


Exterior Stucco Cracking

Florida heat can cause stucco to expand and contract. While small hairline cracks are common, larger cracks can lead to water intrusion and should be professionally sealed by the builder under warranty.


HVAC Imbalance & Ductwork

We often find disconnected ducts or poor airflow in specific rooms. Identifying these issues before the warranty expires ensures your system runs efficiently for years to come.

FAQ: 11-Month Warranty Inspections

When should I schedule my inspection?

We recommend scheduling around the 10th or 11th month. This gives you plenty of time to submit the report to your builder before the 12-month deadline.


Do I need to be present?

It is not required, but we highly recommend it so we can show you our findings in person and explain what the builder should do to fix them.


What happens if the builder refuses to fix an item?

Our reports are based on industry standards and local building codes. Having a professional, third-party report makes it much harder for builders to dismiss valid claims.
